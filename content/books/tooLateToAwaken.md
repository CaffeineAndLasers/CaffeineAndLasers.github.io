Damn, this is the first Žižek book I have actually read, and I can see why he occupies the position he does. Žižek manages to thread the needle so well on so many different topics weaving them into a single explanation. It was fantastic. The extended argument he makes throughout the book is to consider the inverse of the self-fufilling proficy. It may only be possible to address the problems of climate change, rise of facism, etc, by assuming it is already too late, and acting to mitigate. He makes an analogy to the cold war, which the U.S only won because they genuinely believed they were losing. 

If I am being honest with myself, the book probably would have gotten a "⭐" if not for the fact that I listened to it as an audiobook on a long drive, and I think I may need to give it a second read through to fully comprehend his arguments. 

I also had a similar reaction to when I read Kevin Rudd's "The Avoidable War" where I think I need to just stop reading political analysis written during hte Biden administration, since they all end on a hopeful playbook on how to fix all the problems they lay out. And boy have things gotten worse since both of these books were published.
