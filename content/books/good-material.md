It is clear that I don't read much romance, because all of my review is just a mishmash of cliches. But I can help but admire how **honest** the book is. There are so many uncomfortably real moments in it, how the main character takes absolutely everything personally, how when we eventually see from other characters perspectives how toxic his behaviour can get. The way that he feels like a burden on his friends, and that in a way he is kind of right about that feeling. God it is uncomfortable to read and be like "yeah, I have thought that before". 

And this is very "Guy whose favourite Rom Com in 500 Days of Summer Coded" but the book really reminds me of 500 Days of Summer in all the great and complicated ways. Would reccomend.
