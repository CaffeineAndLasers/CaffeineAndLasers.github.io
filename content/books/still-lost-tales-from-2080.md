I was undecided to give this a single 🟩 or a double 🟩🟩. I don't want rating inflation to get out of control. This is Sam O'Nella's (the YouTuber) first attempt at fiction, or published writing in general as far as I understand, and is self-published through Amazon.  I really enjoyed it, his signature humour is on full display in this book. If you like his videos, you'll like the book.

It is a collection of short stories, in a Sci-Fi setting in the not too distant future. To paraphrase him, it's not a utopia or dystopia, some things are worse than now, others are better. 

I think the standout stories are "Eggs for Roman", and "Four little words". The later does a whole lot in not many pages. It is fantastic. I also really enjoyed the way "Being a Nothingess" frames mental illness. Controversially I think "Beat" is the weakest of the stories, this is a shame because it is clearly the most personal of the stories, so it feels kind of shit saying the one where he bears his heart out is kind of boring. It's not bad by any means, just imagine if Trainspotting didn't have Ewan McGregor or the cool soundtrack. 

All in all, I thoroughly enjoyed it, and will pick up his next one in a heartbeat.
