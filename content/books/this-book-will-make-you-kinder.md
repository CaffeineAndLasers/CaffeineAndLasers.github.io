I'll say the nice bits first. The illustrations are great. They are cute, funny, shareable, and good illustrations of the argument Garrett makes. I admit this may make me sound like a child, but the motivation to see the next cute picture did keep me reading page after page. 

Secondly, the metaethics he introduces is genuinely novel, (at least in the pop-philosphy I am exposed to). The expression of Morality as an extension of empathy, is something I have never heard of before, and is an idea I was very keen to continue to explore after the first few chapters. 

Unfortunately (in my opinion) the "applied" section of the book (the last three chapters), gets a bit dull. Garrett takes that guinely novel insight into ethical behaviour and uses it to argue that we should. 
1. Check out privillage
2. Listen to minorities. 

While this is decent advice, it did fall a bit flat for me. This is what we were building to? Just a 21st century leftist 101 course?

This appears to be the first book he has published, (while very accomplished in other areas). I would be very interested to see another book by him where we takes the ideas he lays out and explores them with a bit more depth.
