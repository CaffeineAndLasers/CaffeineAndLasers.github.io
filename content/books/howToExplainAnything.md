I read this with the intention of writing a review for the indieweb bookclub, I was very excited about it, finished it well ahead of time and then just never wrote the review. Oh well, here is my late one. "This book could have been a pamflet". 

Ironically, even though the book was full of advice on how to make your writing more succinct, I found there was a little bit too many personal anecdotes and fluff mixed into the book, especially at the beginning. I think I genuinely would have preferred it as a reference text. In spite of this, the tips and techniques he puts forward is genuinely very useful and something I took a lot of notes on, and I intend to make leberal use of in future essays, presentations, publications and everything else. 

Very useful piece, would highly reccomend everyone reads it. Also reccomend Ros trims it into a much shorter referecne pamflet. Perhaps a very long zine?
