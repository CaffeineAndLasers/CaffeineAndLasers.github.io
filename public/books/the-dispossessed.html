The Dispossessed

by Ursula K. Le Guin

Rating: ⭐

Type: Fiction

Read: April 2026

My Thoughts

Oh my, this was so much better than I expected. (And I cam in with high expectatiuons).

Le Guin manages to thread the needle of optimism without naivety. Like I said in my recent piece to Road Less Read I am enjoying fiction about ambitious and optimistic heros. The world has too much dark, gritty and morally grey characters in real life, so I am appreciating stories which show a world where positive change is possible.

That said, the book isn't ignorant about the work. Even utopia needs maininance, lest it devolve back into the mediocratic bureaucracy which it escaped from.

Le Guin also succeeds where many sci-fi authors fail, in describing made up physics which feels realistic to how physics is actually done, and how physicists actually talk. This is something even Cixin Liu can't fully maintain in Three Body Problem. Her description of temporal physics and simultaneity theory feels absolutly like something that wouyld come out of the theory department of a modern theoretical physics department.

In the philosophical dimension, she does a very good job exploring the "Free-from" vs "Free-to" dichotomy, the social dimension of political power, and the limitations of ideology.

Fantastic book. Going on my "must reccomend" list
